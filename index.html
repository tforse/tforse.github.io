Why web development?

I began learning about web development to expand my skillset at work — probably one of the more common reasons to begin coding. I was studying and reporting on cybercrime, but had very little understanding of web technology and other foundational subjects. My ignorance was my motivation, and I devoted my spare time to learning about everything from the Open Systems Interconnection (OSI) model to basic JavaScript and VBA.

Although everything I learned was interesting, what I connected with instinctively was the practical, problem-solving inherent in coding. It was what I missed as a cyber security consultant. Coding forced me to use a part of my brain that just wasn’t being exercised at work. And ultimately, discovering it led me to Founders and Coders. I can’t say what I would do with the F&C experience, but I know that this skillset is one I will pursue regardless.

What I've been doing...

I am originally from the United States, but I have lived in London for the past six years and have dual citizenship. I spent a year getting an MSc in Security Studies — a political science degree — at UCL, and only found out later how impractical a graduate poli-sci degree can be. Afterwards, I spent six months living in Ecuador and Colombia, easily two of the most beautiful countries I’ve seen, before returning to the UK.

I began looking for work, applying for internships at think tanks, and even for graduate programs with major consultancies and financial firms (narrow escape). Somehow, I fell into a cyber threat intelligence research internship with a risk consultancy based in London Bridge. I began learning about cyber security, how to research and write professional-quality reports, and I began to understand the importance of developing a technical skillset.

I’ve alread described how I began learning to code. Almost two months ago now, I left my work in London, moved to Valencia, Spain, and began studying part-time. I spend my mornings contracting for a cyber security company based in London, and my afternoons working through algorithmic challenges or puzzling out the inner workings of CSS styling (much less intuitive).

Regardless of the outcome of this application, F&C has inspired me to pursue something that I truly enjoy. So, thank you.
